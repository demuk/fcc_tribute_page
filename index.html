Africa's Visionary

Timeline of Thommas Sankara

  • 1940 : He was born on December 21, 1949 in Yako, a northern town in the Upper Volta
    He was born into a Roman catholic family. Marguerite Sankara (died March 6, 2000) and Sambo Joseph Sankara (1919 – August 4, 2006)
    His father fought in the French army during World War II and was detained by the Nazis. Sankara’s family wanted him to become a Catholic priest.

  • 1966 : After basic military training in secondary school in 1966, Sankara began his military career at the age of 19 and a year later was sent to Madagascar
    for officer training at Antsirabe where he witnessed popular uprisings in 1971 and 1972 against the government of Philibert Tsiranana and first read
    the works of Karl Marx and Vladimir Lenin, profoundly influencing his political views for the rest of his life.
